GENDER EQUALITY

The Aim

Sustainable Development Goal 5 (SDG 5) targets achieving gender equality and empowering all women and girls. It aims to, by 2030, end discrimination and violence against women, eliminate harmful practices like child marriage, and value unpaid care and domestic work. It also focuses on ensuring women's full participation in leadership roles, access to sexual and reproductive health, equal rights to economic resources, and enhancing women's empowerment through technology. Progress

Progress towards gender equality is clearly off track. Harmful practices like child marriage and female genital mutilation are decreasing, but not fast enough to keep pace with population growth. Gender parity, especially in women's participation in public life and managerial roles, remains distant. At the current rate, achieving gender parity in managerial positions will take 176 years. Urgent action is needed to challenge biased social norms, eliminate harmful practices, and change discriminatory laws. Increasing women's leadership roles and investments in gender equality are crucial at national, regional, and global levels.

Key Actions

  • Ending All Forms of Discrimination and Violence Based on Gender Internationally

  • Eliminating Harmful Practices like Child and Forced Marriage

  • Valuing Unpaid Work through Public Services and Policies

  • Ensuring Participation by Both Genders and Providing Opportunities for Leadership to All
