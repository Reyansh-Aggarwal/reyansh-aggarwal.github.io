DECENT WORK AND ECONOMIC GROWTH

The Aim

Sustainable Development Goal 8 aims to promote sustained, inclusive, and sustainable economic growth, full and productive employment, and decent work for all. The targets include achieving higher levels of economic productivity through diversification, technological upgrading, and innovation; promoting policies that support job creation and entrepreneurship; improving global resource efficiency in consumption and production; and ensuring decent work conditions. Additionally, the goal focuses on eradicating forced labor, human trafficking, and child labor, as well as protecting labor rights and promoting safe working environments.

Progress

Progress towards SDG 8 faces challenges from COVID-19 aftermath, trade tensions, rising debts in developing nations, conflicts, and geopolitical strains, collectively threatening global economic growth. While labour markets have shown resilience, uneven pandemic recovery, the declining protection of labor rights and emerging vulnerabilities erode social justice prospects. The report foresees a worsening labour market outlook, with higher unemployment and sluggish growth in 2024, exacerbating income inequality and jeopardizing equitable pay for women and decent work for young people. Achieving SDG 8 mandates policies fostering economic growth with a focus on social justice and inclusive employment.

Key Actions

  • Promoting Economic Diversification and Innovation

  • Implementing Policies to Support Job Creation and Entrepreneurship

  • Addressing Child and Forced Labour

  • Improving Labour Conditions

  • Promoting Resource Efficiency

  • Enhancing Global Participation
